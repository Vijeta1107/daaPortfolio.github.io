City Bus Network Optimization

Our project focuses on optimizing city bus networks to improve efficiency, reduce travel time, and minimize congestion using advanced algorithmic techniques. By leveraging graph algorithms (e.g., Dijkstra, Floyd-Warshall), we aim to determine the shortest routes, optimize bus stop placements, and balance passenger load across buses. Real-world examples include Google Maps route optimization and public transit systems like BMTC in Bangalore that use similar algorithms for route and scheduling efficiency.

Problem Space:

As cities experience rapid urbanization, public transportation systems face significant challenges, including overcrowded buses, inefficient routes, increased travel time, and uneven passenger distribution. These issues not only contribute to traffic congestion and higher fuel consumption but also result in poor commuter experiences and increased pollution levels. Our project aims to optimize city bus networks using advanced algorithms, ensuring efficient route planning, reduced travel time, balanced passenger load, and minimal environmental impact, ultimately enhancing urban mobility and sustainability.

City Design Recommendations:

  • Integrate advanced digital infrastructure for seamless connectivity and data sharing across city services.
  • Deploy IoT-based sensors for real-time monitoring of traffic, pollution, and resource consumption.
  • Implement energy-efficient systems such as smart grids and renewable energy sources.
  • Develop sustainable urban mobility solutions, including electric public transport and smart traffic management.
  • Ensure data privacy and cybersecurity to protect citizen information and maintain trust in smart city systems.
